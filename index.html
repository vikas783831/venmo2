How to Set Up Your Venmo Account

Complete guide to creating and verifying your Venmo account

1
Download the Venmo app from the App Store or Google Play
2
Sign up with your email, phone number, or Facebook account
3
Verify your email address and phone number
4
Add and verify your bank account or debit card
5
Set up your security features (PIN, fingerprint, etc.)

How to Request Money on Venmo

Never hesitate to ask for what you're owed with these simple steps

1
Open the Venmo app and tap the "Pay or Request" button
2
Enter the username, phone, or email of the person you're requesting from
3
Enter the amount you're requesting
4
Add a note explaining what the request is for
5
Choose whether to make the request private or share with friends
6
Tap "Request" to send your money request

Frequently Asked Questions

To link your bank account: Go to "Settings" → "Payment Methods" → "Add Bank or Card." You can verify instantly with your online banking credentials or manually with your account and routing numbers. Manual verification may take 1-3 business days.

Payments may show as pending when: (1) The recipient hasn't accepted the payment yet, (2) You're sending to a new recipient who needs to verify their account, (3) There's a security review in progress, or (4) For bank transfers, it takes 1-3 business days to process.

Yes, Venmo uses bank-level encryption and security measures including: data encryption, fraud monitoring, optional PIN protection, and multi-factor authentication. However, only use Venmo with people you trust, as payments can't typically be reversed.

Venmo is free for most personal transactions when using your Venmo balance, bank account, or debit card. There's a 3% fee for credit card payments. Instant transfers to your bank have a 1.75% fee (min $0.25, max $25), while standard transfers are free but take 1-3 business days.

To split a bill: (1) Tap "Pay or Request" → "Split," (2) Select friends to split with, (3) Enter the total amount, (4) Venmo will calculate equal shares (or you can adjust amounts), (5) Add a note (like "Dinner bill"), (6) Tap "Split" to send requests.

Yes, but you need a Venmo business profile. Personal accounts shouldn't be used for business. Business profiles have a 1.9% + $0.10 fee per transaction. To set one up: Go to "Settings" → "Business Profile" and follow the prompts to register.

Your Venmo balance is money stored in your Venmo account that you can send immediately. Bank transfers move money between your Venmo balance and your bank account. Standard transfers are free (1-3 business days), while instant transfers have a small fee (minutes to hours).

To increase your weekly rolling limit beyond $299.99: (1) Verify your identity by providing your legal name, address, date of birth, and SSN (or ITIN), (2) This increases your limit to $4,999.99 per week. Business profiles have different limits.

Immediately: (1) Ask the recipient to refund the payment, (2) If they don't respond, contact Venmo support through the app, (3) For unauthorized transactions, report it within 60 days. Note: Venmo can't guarantee refunds for mistaken payments, so always double-check recipient details.

Venmo currently only works within the U.S. with U.S. bank accounts and phone numbers. While you can access the app abroad, both sender and recipient must have U.S. accounts. The Venmo debit card also only works for U.S. dollar transactions.
